## Iterations

- **iter13 (verified)**: root rec 5's `$INDEX_ROOT` was empty —
  every system record's `$FILE_NAME parent_reference=(5,5)` had no
  return-link in root's `$I30`. chkdsk Stage 2 reported all 12
  records as orphans. Added `build_root_index_root_attr` +
  `RootIndexChild`, sorted under `COLLATION_FILE_NAME` (ASCII upcase

  $Boot, $Extend, $LogFile, $MFT, $MFTMirr, $Secure, $UpCase,
  $Volume, .`). $FILE_NAME copies in entries zero
  `allocated_size/real_size/file_attributes` to mirror reference.

  **Result**: orphan messages gone across every variant; Stage 1 +
  Stage 2 verify clean. Post-Stage-2 reconnect-scan errors with
  `An unspecified error occurred (frs.cxx:60f)`. Diag dirs

  **Result**: Windows rejected the volume at *mount* time

## Iter15+ debug ladder (handed off)

The remaining `frs.cxx:60f` happens during chkdsk's "scanning
unindexed files for reconnect" phase. With root's `$I30` populated
(iter13) and root's SD present (iter14-v2), the most likely
remaining causes are structural mismatches in the system files
chkdsk reaches *via* root's index:

1. **`$Extend` (rec 11) layout mismatch**. Microsoft's reference has
   no `$FILE_NAME` on rec 11 — it's a non-indexed system blob with
   just `$DATA` + `$SECURITY_DESCRIPTOR`. We use rec 11 as `$Extend`
   with a `$FILE_NAME` parented to root. chkdsk may have hardcoded
   knowledge that rec 11 is *not* a directory.
2. **`$Secure` (rec 9) view-index attributes**. Reference has
   `$SDS / $SDH / $SII` populated; ours has only an empty `$DATA`
   stub. chkdsk has hardcoded knowledge of `$Secure`'s structure
   (we already had to set IS_VIEW_INDEX in iter12) and may demand
   the view indexes too.

Recommended approach: byte-diff every attribute in rec 9 and rec 11
against the reference dump (`reference-mft-16recs.bin`) and surface
the next concrete divergence before changing code.
